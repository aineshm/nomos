The problem

Traffic isn't a car problem. It's a coordination problem.

By 2040 the cars drive themselves — so the bottleneck stops being the vehicle and becomes how thousands of them negotiate shared space. Yet there's no good environment to train or verify multi-agent driving at city scale without traffic lights.

  • Reward isn't stationary. Every car you add changes the world for every other car — classic single-agent RL breaks.
  • No shared benchmark. You can't improve what you can't verify, and "did the city run safely?" had no environment to measure it.
  • Signals are a crutch. Lights exist because humans can't coordinate in real time. Autonomous fleets shouldn't need them.
The solution

One policy. Every car. No lights.

A single shared, decentralized RL policy drives every car from its own local view — same brain, separate eyes. It learns to anticipate its neighbors and negotiate intersections by yielding, so the whole city flows without a central controller and without signals.

  • Multi-reward. No crashing ≫ keep moving > route efficiently.
  • Crashes as a constraint, not a penalty — PPO-Lagrangian drives them toward zero instead of trading safety for speed.
  • Deterministic verifier grades every run from the trajectory: crashes, off-road, wrong-way, arrivals. Reward and validity come from the same source of truth.
  • Generalizes to neighborhoods it never trained on.
How it works

Real map → real RL → verified runs.

01

Real San Francisco

The drivable street network — roads, junctions, speed limits — is pulled straight from OpenStreetMap and rendered in 3D with terrain and buildings.

02

Multi-agent RL

Hundreds of agents train in a fast vectorized env on GPU, sharing one decentralized policy with a centralized critic (CTDE) so they learn to coordinate.

03

Verify every run

A pure, deterministic verifier replays the logged trajectory and scores it — turning "did the city run safely?" into a reusable, gradeable RL environment.
